## <a class=anchor id=courses></a> Online stats courses

[Regression methods](https://online.stat.psu.edu/stat501/). A semester course from the University of pennsylvania, with examples in Minitab.

[Analysis of discrete data](https://online.stat.psu.edu/stat504/). A semester course which includes two- and threee-way tables, logistic regression, multinomial logistic regression and Poisson regression.

[Epidemiological research methods](https://online.stat.psu.edu/stat507/). A semester course which includes epidemiological methods, logistic and Poisson regression as well as survival analysis.

[Applied data mining and statistical learning](https://online.stat.psu.edu/stat508/). Includes the classical methods of principal components analysis and lcuster analysis as well as the more modern support vector machines and tree-based methods. The main software used is R.

[Bayesian methods in health economics](https://hstalks.com/playlist/409/bayesian-methods-in-health-economics/?biosci). Five talks by Anthony Hagan of the University of Sheffield. 

[Statistical genetics](https://hstalks.com/playlist/963/statistical-genetics/?biosci). Ten talks by different academics, brought together by David Balding of University College London.

[Statistical Rethinking](https://www.youtube.com/playlist?list=PLDcUM9US4XdNM4Edgs7weiyIguLSToZRI). Youtube videos of a 10-week, 20-lecture  course given in 2019-2020 by David McElreath, based on the first edition of his book [*Statistical Rethinking*], available online through [ANU Library](http://anulib.anu.edu.au).


## <a class=anchor id=textbooks></a> Online textbooks

[Statmethods](http://www.statmethods.net) is more of a resource for implementing methods in R, it assumes that you are familiar with the topics it covers. Its range is wide, from descriptive statistics to multiple regression, dimension reduction methods and bootstrapping.

[Laerd](https://statistics.laerd.com) is more of a resource for implementing methods in SPSS. Some of it is behind a paywall.

## <a class=anchor id=youtube></a> Youtube channels

[Wild About Statistics](https://www.youtube.com/WildAboutStatistics) is the videos for a semester course in introductory statistics at the University of Auckland.

[Dr Nic's maths and Stats](https://www.youtube.com/channel/UCG32MfGLit1pcqCRXyy9cAg) is the Youtube channel of the Statistics Learning Centre. This material is aimed at school children and undergraduates. Much of the material is behind a paywall, but some of the key videos are still freely accessible.
